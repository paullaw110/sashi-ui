## Campaign Formats

### Trade Show Countdown Sequence
Standard cadence:
1. Announcement (5 weeks out)
2. 2 weeks away
3. 1 week away
4. 3 days left
5. "See you tomorrow!" / Final countdown
6. Day-of / live posts

Each stage has:
- Email version
- Social post version(s)
- Sometimes A/B variations

## Visual Identity

### Colors
- **Primary:** Olive/gold (#8B8B3D-ish range)
- **Accent:** Pink/magenta for urgency
- **Background:** White, cream, light sage

### Typography
- Clean sans-serif
- Bold headlines
- Clear hierarchy

### Photography
- Team photos (relationship-focused)
- Product shots on white/neutral
- Food lifestyle (dishes using their products)
- Trade show booth shots

### Layout Patterns
- Dual logo lockup (Pacific Spice + event partner)
- Event info banner (WHAT/WHEN/WHERE or single line)
- Product grid arrangements
- Food collage borders

## Dos and Don'ts

### Do:
- Feature the team — personal connection matters in B2B
- Include clear event details (date, location, booth #)
- Use countdown urgency appropriately
- Partner logo alongside Pacific Spice logo
- Show both product AND application (food shots)

### Don't:
- Be too corporate/stiff — they're warm and approachable
- Forget booth numbers or key details
- Overuse urgency language (save pink/magenta for final days)
- Skip the human element — team photos perform
